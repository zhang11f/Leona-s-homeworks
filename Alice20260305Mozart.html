Grade 6 · Combined Area Problems ·

1
A birthday card is shaped like a rectangle with a triangular flap on top. Find the total area.
Measurements
  • Rectangle: 8 cm × 5 cm
  • Triangle base: 8 cm
  • Triangle height: 3 cm
💡 Calculate each part separately, then add!
Total Area = cm²
2
A wooden shelf has two identical rectangular notches cut from its top corners. Find the remaining area.
Measurements
  • Full rectangle: 14 cm × 9 cm
  • Each notch: 3 cm × 2 cm
  • There are 2 notches
💡 Full area minus both notches!
Remaining Area = cm²
3
A picture frame backing has a trapezoid top section and a rectangular bottom. What is the total area?
Measurements
  • Rectangle: 10 cm × 7 cm
  • Trapezoid bottom base: 10 cm
  • Trapezoid top base: 6 cm
  • Trapezoid height: 4 cm
💡 Don't forget the trapezoid formula: ½ × (a+b) × h
Total Area = cm²
4
A triangular garden has a parallelogram-shaped pond cut out from its centre. Find the area of the garden (not the pond).
Measurements
  • Big triangle base: 16 cm
  • Big triangle height: 12 cm
  • Parallelogram base: 5 cm
  • Parallelogram height: 4 cm
💡 Big triangle area minus the parallelogram pond!
Garden Area = cm²
5
A T-shaped sign is made of two rectangles joined together. What is the total area of the sign?
Measurements
  • Top bar: 12 cm × 3 cm
  • Vertical stem: 4 cm × 10 cm
💡 The stem sits below the bar — no overlap! Add both areas.
Total Area = cm²
6
An arrow shape is made of a parallelogram with a triangle pointing to the right. Find the total area.
Measurements
  • Parallelogram base: 11 cm
  • Parallelogram height: 6 cm
  • Triangle base: 8 cm
  • Triangle height: 5 cm
💡 Calculate each shape, then add. The triangle points right — like an arrow!
Total Area = cm²
